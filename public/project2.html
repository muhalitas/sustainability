Sustainable Manufacturing and Global Supply Chain Management

Globalization of the world economy fastens the separation between the consumption and production spatially. While resource extraction and agricultural production take place in some parts of the word, industrial manufacturing, redistribution, and consumption occur in different regions. Environment, economy, and energy issues become a topic of considerable interest worldwide since world is in the midst of the ecological problems as a result of excessive use of natural resources, steeply rising carbon emissions, and economic crises. Economy, the environment, and energy are fundamentally connected as sustainable manufacturing seeks to profitably produce goods while minimizing the industry’s environmental impacts, natural resource uses, and energy consumption. As a part of various projects at regional and global scales, S3-Lab performs system-based simulations, economic models, and develops wide range of decision-support tools to assist policy-making for sustainable manufacturing.
